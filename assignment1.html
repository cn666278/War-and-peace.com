History

War is an intensive armed conflict between states, political entities, ethnic groups or paramilitary organizations (eg mercenaries, militias ). Wars are characterized by extreme violence, aggression, destruction, and casualties, involving regular or irregular armies. In state-to-state wars, wars are also seen as extreme means of politics and diplomacy , as it is often the politicians rather than the soldiers who trigger the war. In human society, war is often closely related to politics and is an organized armed conflict between multiple organizations and organizations. Clausewitz believes that "war is the continuation of politics". Using force as a means, the purpose is to force one party to obey the will of the other party (usually political intentions), and make the enemy lose the strength of resistance through many large-scale confrontations, such behavior is war. The art of war is the way of fighting in war, and the absence of war is called peace . Some scholars do not agree that war must be related to politics, and some wars appear earlier than politics and countries. Therefore, John Keegan believes that Clausewitz's definition is limited and only applicable to civilized society. Society is culture itself ”. The deadliest war in history was World War II, with between 60 million and 85 million dead.

Art
Some work of art that is critical but philosophical:

I covered the body behind the soldier with the Australian flag and wrapped a corner of the flag around the Afghan boy's head. "I have the boy holding the lamb's baby in his arms," he said. "It contrasts with the color, so that when the reader sees the painting, he will notice the lamb first and then the boy with the knife held to his throat. I also designed an effect of the blue of the Australian flag 'swallowing' the red of the Afghan flag, which is also a kind of allusion to the reality of some countries in Afghanistan, such as the US and Australia." He said his original intention is to fully reflect the reality of the work and cause the audience to reflect. "What I have drawn looks absurd, but it is a real picture of something that happened somewhere in the world. I hope more people will see this painting and pay attention to this tragedy that really happened."
